Interestingly enough, many of the books we consider great classics today were originally published as philosophical works from the Age of Reason. At a time when radical views and nonconformity were considered blasphemy, persecuted philosophers disguised their beliefs as works of fiction, expertly weaving their ideals into popular books of the day.
